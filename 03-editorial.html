On Practice

The elegance of design meets the craft of building.

Great software comes from first principles: find the minimal structure that implies the whole, then make it work in the messiness of the real world. I live in the friction between elegant design and practical engineering — that's where interesting things happen.

Abstraction isn't decoration. It's the necessary compression of complexity into understandable form.

I'm drawn to compilers, type systems, and formal verification — domains where abstraction isn't decoration but necessity. When I'm not designing architectures, I'm building tools that make rigorous thinking easier for everyone else.

Ignacio Gonzalez Secadas
